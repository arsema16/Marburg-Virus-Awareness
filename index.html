About the virus

Marburg virus is a rare but severe hemorrhagic disease. It spreads through contact with infected body fluids and requires urgent medical attention. Outbreaks have occurred in parts of Africa, with the virus related to Ebola and fruit bats as a natural reservoir.

Symptoms

Sudden fever, headache, weakness, muscle pain, abdominal pain, vomiting, diarrhea; can progress to bleeding and shock.

View symptoms

Prevention

Avoid contact with bodily fluids, use PPE when caring for sick individuals, practice hygiene, and report suspected cases.

See prevention tips

Patient advice

Seek medical care immediately, isolate, do not share personal items, and follow health professional guidance.
